Phase alignment under asymmetric delay

Jan 2025 · Status: experimental · Tags: phase, delay, measurement

1. Context

This note investigates the effect of asymmetric delay on phase alignment accuracy in distributed FPGA-based timing systems.

2. Experimental setup

Point-to-point optical link with configurable asymmetry (or controlled path differences), measured at the system level.

3. Method / measurement

Phase is measured using a DDMTD-based approach as a baseline reference. Logging focuses on repeatability, drift, and residual phase error under varying asymmetry.

4. Results

Observed phase error increases with uncompensated asymmetry beyond a practical threshold. (Quantitative results to be added once the measurement sweep is finalized.)

5. Observations / interpretation

Asymmetry introduces a system-level bias that cannot be corrected by phase alignment alone. This motivates explicit asymmetry measurement and compensation, or a model that accounts for it.

6. Next questions

  • Can asymmetry be detected without explicit round-trip measurement?
  • How does this scale in cascaded or meshed topologies?
  • What is the stability limit over time and temperature?
