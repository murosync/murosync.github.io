Concept

Timing as a system parameter

Time is often treated as background infrastructure — assumed to be “good enough” once clocks are distributed. In large distributed systems this assumption breaks down.

Timing quality directly affects correlation, stability, reproducibility, and the physical meaning of measured events. Phase errors, asymmetries, and non-deterministic delays accumulate into system-level behavior that cannot be fixed locally.

Murosync treats time and phase as first-class system parameters, subject to measurement, control, and architectural trade-offs.

Emergent synchronization

The conceptual inspiration behind Murosync comes from emergent synchronization phenomena: global coherence arising from local interactions rather than centralized control.

  • distributed nodes with local timing awareness
  • measurement-driven alignment loops
  • system-level validation rather than isolated optimization
Murosync is research-first: it documents experiments and the constraints discovered along the way.
